# Scroll Patterns

Use these when the page needs richer movement than simple reveal-on-entry.

## 1. Sticky Story

Pattern:

- one visual panel stays pinned
- adjacent steps scroll past it
- the pinned surface changes state as the story advances

Best for:

- walkthroughs
- comparisons
- product explanation

Rules:

- keep the pinned area stable
- change internal content, not the whole shell
- limit the number of steps
- turn off sticky behavior on small screens

## 2. Pinned Product Frame

Pattern:

- the product stays visible while text modules scroll
- proof and annotations attach to the product surface

Best for:

- interface demos
- feature launches

## 3. Rail Movement

Pattern:

- modules slide or stagger within a bounded section
- the section itself still reads as one unit

Best for:

- galleries
- visual systems
- modular proof clusters

Rules:

- keep one visual anchor stable
- do not let the rail feel detached from the page
- use for one major section, not every section

## 4. Layered Stack

Pattern:

- sections or cards overlap slightly
- depth builds as the user scrolls

Best for:

- campaign pages
- narrative launches

## 5. Horizontal Scroll Gallery

Pattern:

- vertical progress drives a horizontal sequence
- cards, frames, or modules move laterally as one section

Best for:

- showcases
- case studies
- visual feature tours

Rules:

- keep the section finite
- maintain orientation with clear captions or labels
- flatten to a simpler stacked layout on mobile

## 6. Static Counterpoint

Pattern:

- a deliberately quiet section between richer motion modules

Best for:

- re-centering the page
- restoring readability
- increasing contrast between motion-heavy sections

## Warnings

Reject the pattern if:

- sticky behavior becomes confusing
- movement overwhelms the content
- mobile collapse feels broken
- the pattern exists only because it looks fancy
- there is no contrast between animated and static sections

# Layout Archetypes

Pick one primary archetype based on the brief. These are layout systems, not themes.

## 1. Editorial Split

Best for:

- launch pages
- premium SaaS
- editorial-feeling brands

Structure:

- oversized narrative block on one side
- proof, product surface, or modular support on the other
- strong left/right tension

Use when the copy needs authority and the page should feel composed rather than busy.

## 2. Asymmetric Bento

Best for:

- feature-heavy products
- AI tools
- dashboards and workflows

Structure:

- varied card sizes
- modular evidence clusters
- one large anchor block with smaller supporting panels

Use when the product has multiple capabilities but still needs a strong visual rhythm.

## 3. Narrative Stack

Best for:

- storytelling pages
- brands with a strong message arc
- product launches with sequential explanation

Structure:

- one strong section at a time
- alternating compression and release
- clear section pacing from hook to proof to CTA

Use when the reading path matters more than scanning.

## 4. Gallery Runway

Best for:

- visual brands
- portfolios
- identity systems
- image-led product pages

Structure:

- staggered showcases
- horizontal moments or framed galleries
- interspersed narrative captions

Use when assets carry a meaningful amount of persuasion.

## 5. Product Frame

Best for:

- software pages that need interface focus
- apps with screenshots or product demos
- feature launch pages

Structure:

- framed product stage
- supporting text and proof orbiting the main surface
- controlled emphasis on product realism

Use when the product UI itself should act as the hero.

## 6. Proof Rail

Best for:

- trust-heavy B2B pages
- enterprise products
- consulting or service offers

Structure:

- narrative block paired with vertical proof strips
- logos, metrics, testimonials, and capability markers placed as a supporting rail

Use when credibility must land early and repeatedly.

## 7. Dense Salon

Best for:

- products with many details
- comparison-heavy pages
- tools aimed at expert users

Structure:

- compact but ordered information zones
- stronger internal grid discipline
- careful contrast between dense and calm sections

Use when information density is required but visual clutter is unacceptable.

## 8. Stage And Satellites

Best for:

- concept-heavy hero sections
- startup launches
- products that need one strong main statement

Structure:

- one dominant stage area
- supporting cards orbiting the primary thesis
- layered visual depth

Use when the page needs a memorable opening frame.

## Choosing Well

Select the archetype by asking:

- Is the page read linearly or scanned modularly?
- Does proof need to appear early or later?
- Is the product itself the visual centerpiece?
- Does the brand need calm authority or energetic movement?

Do not switch archetypes mid-page unless there is a strong narrative reason.

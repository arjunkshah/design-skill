# Component Families

Use these patterns to widen the UI vocabulary without losing layout discipline.

## 1. Bento Clusters

Best for:

- feature-rich products
- AI tools
- modular marketing pages

Use:

- one dominant card
- smaller proof and utility cards around it
- mixed aspect ratios

Avoid:

- six matching cards with different words

Variants:

- static bento with one dominant proof card
- animated bento with one or two alive surfaces
- mixed bento with product, proof, and copy coexisting

## 2. Proof Rails

Best for:

- enterprise pages
- trust-heavy offers
- launch pages needing constant credibility

Use:

- slim side strips
- metric chips
- logo clusters
- testimonial snapshots

## 3. Product Frames

Best for:

- SaaS
- apps
- dashboard tools

Use:

- one main interface surface
- surrounding annotations, chips, or support cards
- a strong visual stage

## 4. Editorial Splits

Best for:

- message-led pages
- creative tools
- premium brands

Use:

- large copy on one side
- modular proof or interaction on the other

## 5. Gallery Runways

Best for:

- visual brands
- portfolios
- image-led launches

Use:

- staggered image frames
- horizontally biased sections
- captions that create pacing

Variants:

- static runway
- horizontally scrolling runway
- pinned runway with changing captions

## 6. Sticky Story Modules

Best for:

- workflow explanation
- feature walkthroughs
- state comparisons

Use:

- one pinned preview
- step content that updates or changes around it
- a finite number of steps with a clear end

Companions:

- framed product stage
- proof rail
- comparison table

## 7. Central Thesis Blocks

Best for:

- major hero moments
- manifesto sections
- chapter resets

Use:

- one large statement
- minimal surrounding noise
- either very quiet motion or none at all

## 8. Horizontal Proof Belts

Best for:

- logos
- testimonials
- benchmark snapshots
- modular feature rails

Use:

- lateral grouping
- repeated units with strong restraint
- horizontal bias without losing section identity

## Rule

Do not use every pattern on every page. Choose three or four compatible families and let one of them dominate.

Residences

Three stay types, each arranged around a different tempo.

This page is not trying to explain a platform. It is trying to create booking confidence through specificity, atmosphere, and a stronger image hierarchy.

Cliff suite

Sea-facing mornings and private dining after dusk.

For longer stays built around horizon views, deep terraces, and quiet entertaining.

Garden house

Family pacing with a calmer interior cadence.

Grounded rooms, shaded exterior spaces, and a more domestic service rhythm.

Studio retreat

Short stays that still feel ceremonial.

For guests arriving around one moment and wanting the whole property to frame it.

House note

The site should make a reservation feel like entering a world, not selecting inventory.

That is why the page uses full-bleed imagery, quieter typography, and fewer modular “features” than the enterprise and consumer examples.

The house ritual

Arrival, morning, and evening are part of the product.

The strongest hospitality pages sell how time will feel on property. This section is about sequencing, not feature count.

01 arrival

Sunset transfer and private check-in.

Guests meet the property through service, not through a front-desk queue.

02 morning

Guided breakfast and shoreline pacing.

The first day is structured to slow the guest down immediately.

03 evening

Reading, dining, and room return as one atmosphere.

The product is the feeling after 6pm, so the site gives that emotional space real weight.
